What We Do

Services

Needs We Serve

  • Dissolution of marriage

    We discuss property and debt division and child custody and support and file the appropriate documents.

  • Child custody

    We provide legal advice and ancillary resources to create a parenting plan that serves your child’s best interest.

  • Prenuptial agreements and postnuptial agreements

    We help you determine how you wish to own assets and cover liabilities into the future.

  • Wills and trusts

    We work with you to determine your needs and desires for the disposition of your assets and the provision of the heirs you choose.

  • Unmarried partnership agreements

    We assist you in determining how you wish to own assets with your domestic partner (co-habitant) and how you wish to pay for joint and separate expenses.

Issues We Resolve

  • Child custody/parenting plan disputes and modification

    We help you make a proposal that is in your child’s best interest and has a basis in law and fact.

  • Child support disputes and modifaction

    We assist you in determining the amount of child support possible under the law and those expenses that need to be covered beyond basic support.

  • Parentage actions

    We assist you in establishing legal parenthood and formulating a parenting plan when that is appropriate.

  • Unmarried partnership agreements

    We assist you in resolving disputes over unmarried partnership agreements and drafting appropriate modifications where possible.

Approaches

  • Mediation

    We mediate issues that cannot be resolved in negotiation and attempt to resolve the issues to avoid the need for court intervention or trial.

  • Negotiation

    We attempt to resolve issues by making reasonable proposals and negotiating resolutions to issues whenever possible.

Results We Achieve

  • Results We Achieve

    We write agreements that can be utilized for legal separations or dissolutions of marriage.

  • Prenuptial and postnuptial agreements

    We write agreements that can withstand the changing circumstances of relationships and meet the standards required to be upheld should there be a legal dispute.

  • Cohabitation agreements

    We write agreements that can serve during a period of cohabitation and can also serve as prenuptial agreements should the parties get married at a later date.

  • Parenting Plans

    We write plans that meet the needs and best interest of the child at each stage of the child’s development and address any problems between parents or with a parent.
